# Orbita Collective — Brand Guidelines

> Source of truth for any task requiring Orbita's visual or verbal style.
> Always apply this system when producing decks, documents, web pages, social posts, proposals, or any other branded artifact.

---

## 1. Brand essence

**Tagline:** *We reinvented consultancy so you could reinvent your products & services.*

**Brand pillars:**
- **Inspiring** — Bringing fresh thinking to complex organisations
- **Impactful** — Building progress you can own, and carry forward
- **Affordable** — To match how the industry has changed

**Promise:** *Everything we deliver, you can use.*

**Domain:** orbitacollective.com

## 2. Logo

### Wordmark + dot
The primary mark is the wordmark **"Orbita"** with a single coral dot positioned at the lower-left of the "O", suggesting a satellite in orbit. This is the visual heart of the brand — the dot must always be present.

### Color variants
- **Dark wordmark on light background** — default, use whenever the canvas is cream or white.
- **Light wordmark on dark background** — for dark/colored backgrounds (navy, charcoal, sky blue).
- The **coral dot stays coral** in every variant.

### "O" monogram
A simplified "O + dot" monogram exists for favicons, social avatars, end slides, and any tight square crop.

### Animated logo
- **Always use V1 of the animated logo. Never use V2.**
- Source: https://drive.google.com/open?id=1mVZwYcHfzT3dzWILmXVB5gk_A07wDA62
- Use the animated version for video intros/outros, web hero loops, and presentation opens — not for static print.

### Logo do's and don'ts
- Do: keep generous clear space (at least the height of the dot on all sides).
- Do: keep the coral dot in its original lower-left orbit position.
- Don't: rotate, recolor, or stretch the wordmark.
- Don't: place the logo on a busy photo without a backing shape.
- Don't: substitute V2 of the animated logo under any circumstance.

## 3. Color palette

Hex values are approximated from the brand swatch sheet. Use these consistently across digital and print.

### Foundations (use most)
| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary background | Cream | `#F1ECDF` | Default canvas for slides, docs, web sections |
| Primary text | Charcoal | `#1F1F1F` | Body copy, headlines on light backgrounds |
| Reverse text | Cream/Off-white | `#F1ECDF` | Headlines and body on dark/colored backgrounds |

### Signature accent (the Orbita coral)
| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero accent | Coral | `#FA8775` | Logo dot, highlight headlines, key CTAs, decorative semicircles, brand callouts |

The **coral is non-negotiable** — it appears on virtually every artifact, even if just as a small decorative element.

### Secondary palette
| Name | Hex | Best used for |
|---|---|---|
| Plum / Mauve | `#6B5B6E` | Service category fills (e.g., "Design OPS"), secondary icons |
| Amber | `#F7B65A` | Tertiary category accent, callouts that need warmth |
| Navy | `#245A82` | Dark backgrounds for "vision" or thought-leadership pages |
| Sky Blue | `#82B9E5` | Background for hero/aspirational slides, secondary fills |
| Slate Blue-Gray | `#B6C2CC` | Subtle accents, dotted lines, low-emphasis chips |
| Peach / Sand | `#F5D6B8` | Background fills for soft category cards |

### Color usage rules
- Cream is the **default** background. Reach for it first.
- Coral is the **only** color that signals "this is Orbita." Use it on every artifact, even subtly.
- Limit any single layout to **2-3 accent colors max** to keep the palette feeling considered, not crowded.
- For dark variations, **Sky Blue** (`#82B9E5`) is the preferred non-white background — see the "Inspiring/Impactful/Affordable" slide as the template.

## 4. Typography

### Primary font — **Poppins** (all weights)
Poppins is the only typeface in the system. Geometric, clean, friendly, modern.

| Use | Weight | Size guidance | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Bold (700) | 48–80pt | Tight tracking, often 1–3 words |
| Section title | Bold (700) | 28–40pt | Often paired with a thinner subtitle |
| Subtitle / kicker | Regular (400) | 18–24pt | Sits under or above section titles |
| Body | Regular (400) | 14–18pt | Generous line-height (~1.4–1.6) |
| Caption / footer | Regular (400) | 10–12pt | Used for `orbitacollective.com` footer |
| Highlight / pull quote | Bold (700) in **Coral** | 20–28pt | The signature "this matters" treatment |
| Italic accents | Italic (400 or 700) | varies | Used for short labels like *Customer Experience* |

### Type rules
- **Never** mix in another typeface. Poppins handles everything.
- Use **Bold + Coral** to call out the single most important sentence on a page (see About Us deck for examples).
- Body copy is **Charcoal on Cream** — always. Avoid mid-grays for body text.
- Web/CSS fallback stack: `'Poppins', 'Inter', 'Helvetica Neue', Arial, sans-serif`.

## 5. Layout & composition

Patterns to follow, drawn from the About Us deck.

### Canvas
- Cream background, full-bleed.
- Generous margins — let things breathe. Aim for ~6–8% page padding minimum.
- Footer on every page: small `orbitacollective.com` centered at the bottom.

### Grid
- **2-column** or **3-column** are the workhorses (e.g., founder bios, three service offerings, "Reinventing by:" tracks).
- Headline + supporting paragraph on the **left**, image or visual element on the **right** is the default content slide.

### Decorative motif — the orbit
Circles and semicircles are the brand's signature decorative element (the satellite metaphor). Use them sparingly to add rhythm:
- A **coral semicircle** peeking in from a slide edge (see "founders" slide).
- **Filled colored circles** as category badges (coral, plum, amber, sky blue) with the label inside.
- A **thin curved line orbiting** a circle (see "Service & experience design" — the orbital ring).
- A small **lone coral dot** as visual punctuation.

### Imagery
- Photos use **rounded corners** (radius ~16–24px on a slide-scale image).
- Prefer authentic workshop / team / human imagery over stock.
- Backgrounds in photos should feel calm — neutral walls, real environments.

### Iconography
- Simple flat icons inside a colored circle (coral, plum, sky blue).
- White line/glyph icons inside the colored circle.
- Match the icon circle color to the category tone.

## 6. Voice & tone

### Voice attributes
- **Direct** — say the thing. Short sentences. Active voice.
- **Confident, not arrogant** — we know our craft, we don't perform it.
- **Plain-language** — no consultancy jargon. If a sentence sounds like a McKinsey deck, rewrite it.
- **Warm and human** — we're collaborators, not vendors.
- **Modern** — use contractions ("we're", "don't").

### Signature phrases (reuse and remix)
- "We reinvented consultancy so you could reinvent your products & services."
- "Everything we deliver, you can use."
- "A simple offering that keeps the value and lets go of the baggage."
- "We work with the ones who don't just want to improve. They want to lead change from the inside-out."
- "Not theoretical frameworks for the sake of it…"

### Words to use
*reinvent · own · usable · inside-out · lead · simple · fresh · impact · affordable · craft · seamless*

### Words to avoid
*synergize · leverage · paradigm · best-in-class · world-class · cutting-edge · disruptive · deep dive · circle back · holistic*

### Punctuation & style
- **British English** spelling (organisation, behaviour, optimise) — confirmed by deck copy.
- Use the ampersand (`&`) in titles ("Design & Innovation", "Product & Service Design").
- Sentence case for body and most headlines; Title Case acceptable for category labels.
- Use em dashes sparingly. Prefer short sentences over long ones with dashes.

## 8. Asset checklist for any new artifact

Before delivering, verify:

- [ ] Cream `#F1ECDF` background (or an approved alt: navy, sky blue, charcoal)
- [ ] Poppins used everywhere — no other fonts
- [ ] Coral `#FA8775` appears at least once (logo dot, highlight, semicircle, or accent)
- [ ] Charcoal body text on light backgrounds; cream text on dark backgrounds
- [ ] Logo present — wordmark + dot, with the dot in lower-left of the "O"
- [ ] If animated, V1 only (never V2)
- [ ] `orbitacollective.com` footer on multi-page documents
- [ ] No jargon, no buzzwords, plain-language voice
- [ ] British English spelling
- [ ] Generous whitespace; no crowding
